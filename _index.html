How to...

Win with this retailer Retain my most valuable shoppers Grow the category Improve brand shopper marketing effectiveness Optimise point of purchase efficiency

  • What does my category deliver to this retailer?

    • What is its role: to bring in Traffic or to drive Spend?

      Higher scores from shoppers on one or more of the "Traffic" measures indicate an ability to impact the trip before the store, whereas higher scores on "Spend" measures show impact once in the store.

    • Is it high or low engagement in this retailer?

      Shoppers tell us whether they intended this to be a high speed or more considered purchase (Grab and Go/Browse). And then if something changed this in store (either positively or negatively in their view).

    • Where does it sit in the Intentionality/Engagement Matrix?

      The 4 box matrix summarises the overall DNA of the category; identifying its position in the mix of the store for this retailer; high ratings on Intentionality and Engagement make this a more attractive category to invest in for the retailer. Each quadrant implies a different strategy.

    • Does this vary for different shopper types? Or for our brand versus others?

      Does the category role vary by age group, by income, by gender of shoppers?

    • How does our brand compare on the role implied by its shoppers?

      Do our brand shoppers show a different role profile? If so, our brand may be able to move the dial for the retailer.

    • How does this differ to other banners/channels?

      Is there anything unique about this retailer in the way the category operates?

  • What’s different about this retailer's shoppers?

    • Who shops at this retailer? (demographics, needs, behaviours)

      Are there certain shopper types that are more likely to shop at this retailer across its entire business, or within our "Department"? We need to think about orienting our marketing to suit their target market.

    • What Missions are this retailer's shoppers more likely to be on?

      Does this retailer show a prevalence of bigger or smaller shopping trips? How should our category play to that?

    • What do this retailer’s shoppers want?

      Do this retailer's shoppers want particular things? More than other banners? Particularly in our Department/category?

    • Is our category behaving differently at this retailer?

      What is the pattern of choices in this retailer compared to others, in our category?

    • Are certain shopper needs more prevalent for our category at this retailer?

      Are there occasions (users, times) that are more common for this retailer than others; in the Department or in our category?

  • Where is this retailer winning/losing?

    • Where does this retailer do better or worse than its key competitors?

      Looking at the store and this Department, how does it benchmark in shopper satisfaction on all measures to competitor banners? What are its strengths and weakenesses.

    • Where does this retailer do better or worse than its key competitors in 'net recommend' ratings?

      Looking at the store and this Department: How does it benchmark in Overall (net) recommend rating to competitor banners? What are its strengths and weaknesses?

    • Is this particularly true of certain shoppers?

      Are certain types of shoppers more satisfied? Or do they want particular improvements?

    • What improvements are shoppers at this retailer looking for?

      Looking at the store and this Department: Are shoppers in this retailer looking for particular improvements? Is this different to the norm?

    • How is their private label performing?

      How does Private Label perform in this retailer; compared to competitors overall; in this Department?

    • Do they have a strong Private Label image?

      How willing are shoppers to risk choosing Private Label in this retailer? How does this compare?

  • How do I refocus my portfolio for success?

    • Which segments/brands are the opportunity for growth in this retailer? So how do I win support for them?

      Do some brands in the category work harder for the store, i.e. are the rated higher on intentionality or engagement? If so, can this be leveraged?”
